The Staged Learning Journey

Excellent training requires progression. Wellbeing Builder moves learners from shared language, through guided practise, to personal integration. Each stage builds capacity for deep, lasting change.

1
Stage 1

Foundational Concepts

Core ideas like identity, context, resources, and action are introduced through learning. The focus is clarity over complexity, building a shared language for wellbeing practise.

Establishes the conceptual framework for all practise that follows.

2
Stage 2 Core Practise

Guided Practise with Scaffolding

AI-supported scenarios invite learners to practise noticing what is really happening. Support is strongest at the start and gradually fades as confidence grows.

Skills broken into manageable moves

Feedback linked to insight, not judgment

Cases calibrated just beyond comfort zone

3
Stage 3

From Practise to Real-Life Application

Each learning run concludes with a personal action plan. Learners commit to testing specific actions in their own context and observing the change. Reflection connects these actions with personal values and long-term direction, focusing on sustainable capacity that transfers to study, work, and life.

Focuses on sustainable identity-level change that transfers to real life.

Why most wellbeing training fails

The Problem

  • Inspiration without practise: Motivational talks fade within days
  • Theory without application: You learn concepts but don’t know how to use them
  • Quick fixes that do not transfer: One-off workshops don’t stick across different contexts
  • Disconnected from real life: Generic advice misses your actual challenges and resources

The Solution

Wellbeing Builder treats wellbeing as a learnable skill set, not just a feeling. Practise in realistic scenarios. Reflect with AI support to reveal patterns. Test actions in your own life. Integrate insights into who you are.

The result is sustainable capacity that transfers across study, work, and personal transitions.

Join the early research pilot

Wellbeing Builder is in early beta and currently being tested with small groups of young adults, particularly healthcare students navigating demanding training and life transitions.

Important: This is not a commercial product. It is an active research project exploring how wellbeing can be learned and practised as a real skill.

Who we are inviting

  • Young adults aged 18 to 30
  • Healthcare students in high-pressure environments
  • Individuals curious about understanding themselves in context

What participation involves

  • Completing short scenario-based wellbeing sessions
  • Completing 3 brief questionnaires to help us understand the impact of the practise
  • Contributing to a pioneering study on AI-supported wellbeing learning
